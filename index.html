Dressing the Part

Formal first, with a quiet touch of the enchanted forest. The magic lives in the details — a leaf pin, a jewel-toned dress, a hint of gold. Think fairytale, never costume. One or two accents is plenty.

The Foundation

A composed, formal base

  • Dresses & gowns: A-line, fitted, or softly flowing in chiffon, satin, or soft velvet — fabrics that catch light gently rather than sparkle.
  • Colours: pick any jewel or soft tone above — emerald, teal, plum, sapphire, dusty rose, lilac, gold. One rich shade reads more elegant than many.
  • For the gentlemen: a deep green, teal, plum, or navy suit or waistcoat; a crisp shirt; an optional velvet blazer for a little fairytale drama.
  • Keep it comfortable: we are on our feet through a long morning — choose hemlines and shoes you can move, host, and celebrate in.

The Accents

Where the theme comes alive

  • Gold or brass leaf pins & brooches: a ginkgo, oak, fern, or maple leaf on a lapel, collar, or shawl — the simplest nod to the forest.
  • Hair vines, leafy pins, or a sprig of greenery: tucked into a bun, braid, or loose waves. Subtle, not a full flower crown.
  • Pearl, crystal, or butterfly touches: delicate earrings, a fine pendant, or a tiny butterfly pin for a dewy, morning-mist shimmer.
  • For the gentlemen: a small leaf or eucalyptus boutonnière, or a green or gold pocket square. One detail is enough.

A gentle rule of thumb: subtle shimmer over sparkle, one or two accents rather than many, and always comfortable and yourself. The goal is for teachers to look polished and quietly part of the magic — so the children stay the stars of the day.
